# FUNNEL.md

This document explains the conversion strategy behind the Stellr Media landing page — why each element exists, why it's placed where it is, and what psychological job it's doing.

---

## The Core Premise

The visitor is a real estate agent who clicked a paid ad. They are:
- **Skeptical** — they've been burned by agencies before (this is explicitly called out in the pain section)
- **Busy** — they don't have time to read long pages
- **Mobile** — most are on their phone between showings or in their car
- **Results-oriented** — they care about listings and GCI, not marketing theory

The entire page is built around one conversion: getting them to book a 30-minute strategy call. Every element either builds trust, overcomes an objection, or creates urgency to act now.

---

## The Funnel Stages

### Stage 1 — Stop the Scroll (Urgency Banner + Hero)

The very first thing the visitor sees before anything else is the urgency banner: **"Only 3 markets still available this month."** This does two things simultaneously:
1. Communicates exclusivity (1 agent per market is a core value prop)
2. Creates immediate loss aversion — they might already be too late

The hero headline **"We Get Real Estate Agents 20–40 Qualified Seller Appointments Every Month"** is a specific, outcome-focused promise. It doesn't say "we improve your marketing" — it says exactly what the agent gets. Specificity is credibility.

The sub-headline directs them straight to the VSL: "Watch the short video below." This prevents them from bouncing before they've consumed the core sales message.

---

### Stage 2 — The VSL (The Real Sales Engine)

The VSL is the most important element on the page. Everything else is support material for it.

**Why above the fold:** On a paid ad funnel, the visitor arrived because a video or ad caught their attention. A VSL continues that momentum. Burying it below text would break the flow and lose them.

**The "Watch Before Scrolling" label** creates a soft commitment. It signals that the rest of the page will make more sense after they watch — which is true, and which keeps them engaged longer.

**The 8,400+ agents watched + ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐** below the video provides micro-social proof at the exact moment of hesitation before clicking play. It answers "is this worth my time?" before they even ask.

**Post-roll hook:** When the video ends, a dark overlay appears — "Ready to claim your market?" with a direct booking CTA — and the page auto-scrolls to booking after 2.8 seconds. This captures the visitor at their highest-intent moment: right after they've watched the full pitch and are still in that mental state.

---

### Stage 3 — Market Availability Checker (Hero Bottom)

Placed at the bottom of the hero, after the CTA button. This is a **micro-commitment device**.

The psychology: asking someone to check their market before asking them to book a call is a much lower-friction first step. Once they type their city and see "✅ [City] is still available!", two things happen:
1. They've taken an action — even a small one creates investment
2. The "available" result creates a window that feels closable: *this could be gone*

The result always shows available because every market genuinely is available until someone claims it. The link inside the result takes them straight to booking.

---

### Stage 4 — Instant Social Proof (Testimonials, moved above stats)

Testimonials appear **immediately after the hero** — before stats, before pain points, before anything else.

**Why here:** The visitor just watched a video making big promises. The natural next thought is "sounds good, but is it real?" Testimonials answer that question instantly, while their guard is still down from watching the video. If we made them scroll through stats and pain points first, skepticism would have time to build.

**The screenshot placeholders** sit below the text testimonials. Real screenshots — WhatsApp messages from clients, ad dashboard results, Google reviews — are orders of magnitude more believable than designed quote cards. Text testimonials are polished; screenshots are raw and harder to fake.

---

### Stage 5 — Social Proof by Numbers (Stats Bar)

The stats bar (47 partners, $4.2M GCI, 4.8× ROI, 14 days to first leads) comes **after** testimonials, not before.

**Why this order matters:** Numbers without trust are just numbers. Once the visitor has read three testimonials from real agents, the stats become proof points that validate what they just read — rather than unverifiable claims they read cold. Trust is established first; numbers amplify it.

The **counting animation** reinforces that these are real, live metrics — not static copy that's been sitting there since 2022.

---

### Stage 6 — Pain Agitation

The pain section ("The Marketing Trap Most Real Estate Agents Are Stuck In") serves a specific psychological function: **it makes the visitor feel seen.**

Real estate agents are often reluctant to admit their marketing isn't working. When they read "Feast-or-Famine Pipeline" or "Agency Disappointments" and think *that's exactly me* — we've created alignment. They feel understood, not sold to.

The pain section also **reframes their situation**. The problem isn't that they're bad at their job — it's that they're stuck in a trap that affects most agents. This reduces shame and increases openness to a solution.

---

### Stage 7 — Solution + Case Studies

The solution section and case studies work together as a **logical bridge** from pain to action.

The solution section explains the mechanism — what we actually do and why it works for real estate specifically. The "We *only* work with real estate agents" line is a differentiator that directly counters the "agency disappointments" pain point.

The case studies provide outcome proof: real dollar amounts ($380K GCI, 4.9× ROAS), real brokerages (Keller Williams, RE/MAX, Compass), real markets. The specificity of the numbers does more than any general claim.

---

### Stage 8 — How It Works (Reducing Complexity Fear)

The 3-step process section exists to answer a common unspoken objection: *"This sounds complicated and time-consuming."*

By reducing everything to 3 simple steps — Book Call → We Build It → Pipeline Fills — we eliminate the fear of commitment. The CTA at the bottom of this section ("Get Started — Book Your Call →") catches visitors who are now warm enough to act without needing the full page.

---

### Stage 9 — Guarantee (Risk Reversal)

The 90-day guarantee is placed **deep in the page**, not at the top. This is intentional.

**Why not at the top:** Leading with a guarantee before trust is built can signal desperation or feel like a gimmick. By the time the visitor reaches it here, they already believe the product works. The guarantee at this point removes the **last remaining objection** — financial risk — rather than being the primary sales argument.

"20 qualified seller appointments in 90 days or we keep working for free" is structured to be specific and unambiguous. Vague guarantees ("we'll make it right") create more uncertainty than they resolve.

---

### Stage 10 — FAQ (Objection Handling)

The FAQ handles the five most common objections in order of likelihood:

1. **How quickly will I see results?** — impatience / skepticism
2. **Will you work with other agents in my market?** — competitive concern
3. **How is this different from other agencies?** — "I've been burned before"
4. **What do I need to provide?** — fear of complexity/time commitment
5. **What's the minimum commitment?** — fear of being locked in

Pricing is deliberately **not** in the FAQ. Introducing price before the call creates a decision point the visitor isn't equipped to make — they don't know their market size, potential ROI, or what's included. Every dollar figure they imagine will be higher than reality, and they'll bounce. The call is where price makes sense.

---

### Stage 11 — Booking Section (The Conversion Point)

The booking section is the final destination. Everything above it has been building to this moment.

**Scarcity bar** ("8 of 11 markets claimed — May 2026") appears directly above the calendar. This is where scarcity lands hardest — at the exact decision moment, not buried at the top of the page where it's forgotten by the time they scroll down. The progress bar animates in on scroll to draw the eye.

The copy beneath the progress bar reinforces the mechanism: *"Once your area is gone, the next agent in your market gets the exclusivity instead of you."* This is not manufactured fear — it's a real feature of how the service works. Loss aversion to a specific, named competitor is more motivating than abstract scarcity.

The **GHL calendar** is embedded inline so the visitor never leaves the page to book. Every redirect is a drop-off opportunity. Keeping the calendar on-page removes that friction entirely.

---

### Stage 12 — Floating CTA Button (Persistent Bottom Bar, Mobile)

On mobile only, a fixed "Book Free Strategy Call →" button floats at the bottom of the screen throughout the entire page experience.

**Why mobile only:** Desktop users have a nav button always visible. Mobile users scroll aggressively and the nav disappears. The floating button ensures there is always a booking CTA visible without requiring the visitor to scroll back up or search for it.

## Conversion Principles This Page Is Built On

| Principle | Where it appears |
|---|---|
| **Specificity = credibility** | Headline promise (20–40 appointments), stats, case study dollar amounts |
| **Micro-commitments** | Market checker before booking ask |
| **Social proof sequencing** | Testimonials immediately post-VSL before any pitch |
| **Loss aversion** | Urgency banner, market checker result, scarcity bar |
| **Risk reversal** | 90-day guarantee placed after desire is built |
| **Objection pre-emption** | Pain section, FAQ |
| **Friction removal** | Inline GHL calendar, floating mobile CTA |
| **Peak-moment capture** | VSL post-roll overlay auto-scrolls to booking |
